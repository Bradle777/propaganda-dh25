Analysis

Basic observations

Making broad conclusions based off of the current marked up data would be presumptuous, however some trends are visible. The overall length of each State of the Union is increasing. Healthcare and the economy are hot issues compared to wider social issues such as those concerning LGBT+ and "wokeness" or foreign diplomacy. On that note, domestic policy is mentioned significantly more than foreign policy; it makes sense for a "State of the Union". Immigration and the military were especially hot button topics in the last State of the Union compared to any other point graphed.

Crime has been a hot button topic with Trump's last SOTU, but it's still seldomn mentioned as a major issue in America. Diplomacy is another sparcely mentioned topic, with Biden's 2023 SOTU having many mentions due to the War in Ukraine. Obama's usage being in reference to the legacy of the war on terror and desire to build alliences.

The economy and healthcare are the most mentioned topics when it comes to American SOTUs. Biden's Covid rebuilding and Trump's Big Beautiful Bill are the latest examples, but Obama's plan to recover from the recession also comes up. Health is also mentioned alongside education (a public health issue) in a simular vain in his 2021 and 2023 speeches, but not so much by Trump's last one. Obama's usage of health may seem low, but the ACA is something more focued on outside the graph's current scope.

Immigration is a big topic with Donald Trump. The military is another topic popular with the 47th president, however is also repeated frequently by Obama due to his handling of the war on terror and further threats of terror happening in Europe at that time. Biden doesn't mention much in his speeches due to the priority being on giving military aid to Ukraine.

Party Agenda is a loose term used to describe aspirational statements driven more by ideology than actual policy plans. Its usage has increased over the years, but Obama's speech in 2011 starts with a jump. Social issues refer to things concerning intersectionality. Biden voices a lot of support for trans people in 2021 and 2023, however Trump complains about "wokeness" in 2025. However social issues are underrepresnted overall.

Domestic and foreign policy are somewhat proportional (with exception to Biden 2023) but the SOTU is most certainly overwhelmingly a place for the discussion of domestic policy.
