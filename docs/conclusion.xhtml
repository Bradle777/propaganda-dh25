Forming Conclusions

The core of this project is to create a tool with which one can generate novel conclusions.

While we have presented statistical overlap between attributes in the Analysis section, those patterns
serve more as a standard to identify consistent patterns across the 43-year corpus.

Below, we describe three major lenses through which to interpret rhetoric patterns and form conclusions.

Political Relativist View

Follows the logic that political propaganda is mostly in response to the contemporary political climate.

Economic Messaging in the Early-to-Mid 2000s

Here, it seems obvious that economic optimism would rise after a recession. However, the Political Relativist view also reconciles the lack of economic interest before the recession. We see that political messaging can be interpreted as a gauge of contemporary climate, and thus as a predictor of the future climate.



Social Issue Messaging in the Early-to-Mid 2000s

In less quantifiable areas like Social Issues, a purely political view yields diminishing returns. We can see some increased social optimism as the Nation moves away from the chaotic start of the 2nd Gulf War, but the correlation (and thus the model's ability for prediction) is not as strong or consistent.

Anthropological View

A purely anthropological approach reframes presidential propaganda not as a response to specific historical events, but as a repeated negotiation of fundamental group dynamics. From this perspective, the subject matter of a speech becomes secondary to the structural problem every leader must solve: how to legitimize authority by managing fear vs. optimism, responsibility vs. and blame. This view has the advantage of exposing rhetorical patterns that persist across administrations and eras, such as the consistent use of escalation to create urgency or ritualized resolution to signal control. In the Trump 2018 example, for instance, the escalation pairing does not depend on immigration as a policy domain; rather, it demonstrates how historical harm is compressed into present danger and then stabilized without closure, preserving the leader’s role as an indispensable actor.

The strength of this approach lies in its ability to explain why similar rhetorical moves appear even when political conditions differ dramatically. Leaders must present action as both necessary and uniquely within their capacity to deliver. The Obama 2009 threat–resolution ritual illustrates this clearly: past economic mismanagement establishes danger, present authority asserts the capacity to act, and future benefits finalize legitimacy. Anthropologically, this sequence satisfies a basic group expectation that threats are acknowledged, addressed, and symbolically closed regardless of the specific economic realities on the ground.

The anthropological view also has limitations. By abstracting rhetoric into recurring mechanisms, one risks flattening meaningful political differences and underestimating the role of context. While escalation pairing and threat–resolution rituals can be identified across speeches, this lens alone cannot explain why certain policy domains dominate at particular moments, nor can it reliably predict shifts in emphasis without reference to external conditions. In this sense, the anthropological approach excels at revealing how persuasion works, but not always why it is used.

Example 1: Escalation Pairing: Trump 2018

Text: For decades, open borders have allowed drugs and gangs to pour into our most vulnerable communities...
Attributes: Domestic + Immigration + Negative + Past
 Baseline Threat Through Historical Harm

▼

Text: Here tonight are two fathers and two mothers... MS-13 have been charged with [their] murder.
Attributes: Domestic + Immigration + Negative + Present
 Escalation to Immediate Danger

▼

Text: Tonight, I am calling on the Congress to finally close the deadly loopholes that have allowed criminals to break into our country.
Attributes: Domestic + Immigration + Neutral + Present
 Danger remains real and response is escalated



Example 2: Threat-Resolution Ritual: Obama 2009

Text: We have lived through an era where too often, short-term gains were prized over long-term prosperity...
Attributes: Domestic + Economy + Negative + Past
 Threat Identification through Criticism of the Past

▼

Text: Now is the time to act boldly and wisely – to... revive this economy
Attributes: Domestic + Economy + Neutral + Present
 Transitions Past Negativity to Present Urgency of Resolution

▼

Text: As soon as I took office, I asked this Congress to send me a recovery plan by President’s Day that would put people back to work and put money in their pockets.
Attributes: Domestic + Party Agenda + Positive + Past
 Legitimization of Capability Through Prior Threat-Resolution

▼

Text: Over the next two years, this plan will save or create 3.5 million jobs.
Attributes: Domestic + Economy + Positive + Future
 Liken Past Threat-Resolution to Future Improvements
