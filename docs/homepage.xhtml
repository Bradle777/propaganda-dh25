Presidential Propaganda Project

By: Tiberio Cariccho, Luis Castro, Bradley Evans

President George W. Bush delivers his State of the Union address to the nation and a joint session of Congress in the House Chamber at the U.S. Capitol Tuesday, Jan. 28, 2003.

Overview

Defining propaganda as a message to reinforce or change others political beliefs, what have been different presidents approaches to propaganda

Corpus

Presidential Speech

Research Questions

  • How has the discourse on different topics evolved with context to importance and how have they been prioritized by different parties and presidents?
  • How have different presidents conveyed messages and how has congress responded?
  • Does speech length relate to the address of many issues or an in-depth focus on one issue?
  • How has the amount of time spent focused on a hot topic changed over the years?
