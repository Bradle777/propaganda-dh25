Presidential Propaganda Project

By: Tiberio Cariccho, Luis Castro, Bradley Evans

Background Image:
President Barack Obama delivers his State of the Union address to the Nation and a joint session of Congress in the House Chamber at the U.S. Capitol Tuesday, Jan. 25, 2011.

Overview

We aim to analyse what have been different American Presidents' approaches to propaganda, and how those strategy changes unfolded over time.
We are defining propaganda as a message to reinforce or change others political beliefs.

Corpus

We analyze the annual State of The Union Adress given by the President. Our corpus spans the years 1982 to 2025.

Research Questions

  • How has the discourse on different topics evolved with context to importance and how have they been prioritized by different parties and presidents?
  • How have different presidents conveyed messages and how has congress responded?
  • Does speech length relate to the address of many issues or an in-depth focus on one issue?
  • How has the amount of time spent focused on a hot topic changed over the years?
